# Problem Statement - India's Stray Dog Crisis

## The Crisis

India is facing a worsening stray dog crisis with **26.7 lakh (2.67 million) dog bite cases reported in 2025**. This represents a significant public health emergency that demands immediate attention and systematic intervention.

## Root Causes

### 1. Fragmented Reporting Systems

**Current State:**
- Multiple disconnected reporting channels (police, hospitals, municipal corporations)
- No centralized database of incidents
- Manual, paper-based reporting in many areas
- Lack of standardized incident documentation

**Impact:**
- Delayed emergency response
- Incomplete incident data
- Difficulty in tracking patterns
- Poor resource allocation

### 2. Poor Coordination

**Current State:**
- Hospitals, animal control, and authorities operate in silos
- No real-time communication between stakeholders
- Unclear responsibility and accountability
- Delayed information sharing

**Impact:**
- Slow emergency response times
- Duplicate efforts and wasted resources
- Gaps in service delivery
- Frustrated citizens and healthcare workers

### 3. Preventable Rabies Deaths

**Current State:**
- Anti-rabies vaccines are available but not always accessible
- Lack of awareness about vaccination schedules
- No systematic follow-up for multi-dose vaccines
- Delayed treatment due to poor coordination

**Impact:**
- Preventable deaths despite available vaccines
- Incomplete vaccination courses
- Increased healthcare costs
- Loss of public trust in the system

## Statistics & Impact

### Health Impact
- **26.7 lakh dog bite cases** in 2025 (and rising)
- **Thousands of rabies deaths** annually (preventable)
- **High treatment costs** for victims and healthcare system
- **Psychological trauma** for victims, especially children

### Economic Impact
- **Healthcare costs** for treating dog bites and rabies
- **Lost productivity** due to illness and treatment
- **Emergency response costs** for animal control
- **Legal and administrative costs** for incident management

### Social Impact
- **Fear and anxiety** in communities with high stray dog populations
- **Reduced outdoor activities** especially for children
- **Conflict** between animal welfare advocates and public safety concerns
- **Erosion of trust** in government response systems

## Current System Failures

### 1. Reporting Challenges
- Citizens don't know where to report incidents
- Multiple phone numbers and offices create confusion
- No acknowledgment or tracking of reports
- Language barriers in diverse communities

### 2. Response Delays
- Average response time: **Several hours to days**
- No prioritization based on severity
- Lack of real-time location tracking
- Insufficient animal control personnel

### 3. Data Gaps
- No comprehensive incident database
- Unable to identify hotspots
- Cannot track trends over time
- Difficult to measure intervention effectiveness

### 4. Coordination Issues
- Hospitals don't know about nearby incidents
- Animal control unaware of repeat offender dogs
- NGOs work independently without coordination
- Authorities lack real-time visibility

### 5. Vaccination Challenges
- Patients miss follow-up doses
- No automated reminders
- Vaccine availability not tracked
- No centralized patient records
